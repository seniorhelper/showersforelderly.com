Low Threshold Showers vs Standard Tubs — What Seniors Should Know

← Back to Home

For seniors and older adults, stepping over a high tub wall or dealing with slippery surfaces during bathing can be a major hazard. According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), falls are the leading cause of injury among adults 65 and older. Bathrooms are especially risky because most falls happen when entering or exiting a tub or shower. (CDC)

Why tubs can be risky

  • High tub walls require lifting legs
  • Wet surfaces become slippery
  • Hard to stabilize without grab bars
  • Often need caregiver assistance

Benefits of low threshold showers

  • Lower entry makes stepping in easier
  • Compatible with walkers or wheelchairs (depending on design)
  • Grab bars + seating improve confidence
  • Easier to clean and use daily
  • Can increase home value with modern look

If soaking or hydrotherapy is important, a walk in tub has advantages — and some homes install both for flexibility.
