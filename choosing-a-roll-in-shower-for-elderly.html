Choosing a Roll In Shower for Elderly Loved Ones

← Back to Home

For many seniors and older adults, traditional tubs and high-curb showers pose a serious risk for slips, falls, or difficulty entering and exiting. The bathroom is one of the most dangerous rooms in the home: studies show that falls among older adults are common and often happen during bathing, showering or getting in/out of tubs or showers. (Medical News Today)

Why roll in showers make sense

Roll in (or curbless / barrier-free) showers eliminate the step-over barrier — which reduces trip and fall risk significantly. (Affordable Walk In Shower) Add a textured, non-slip floor, sturdy grab bars, and a shower seat and you get a bathing space designed for mobility limitations and balance challenges. (Apex Windows & Bath)

Key things to check before ordering or installing

  • Threshold height & entry type — ideally zero-entry or very low curb (< ½ inch)
  • Drain style — trench drains or linear drains for smoother rolling
  • Floor texture — non-slip surfaces matter
  • Grab bars & seating — essential safety features
  • Clear floor space for turning mobility devices
  • Lighting & control placement — hand-held wands and reachable controls

When a roll in shower is the right choice

  • Mobility limitations
  • Balance concerns
  • Walker or wheelchair use
  • Assistance from a caregiver
  • Want fast, easy bathing

If someone prefers soaking and hydrotherapy, a walk in tub might still be ideal. Many homes combine both solutions — roll in shower for daily use, walk in tub for therapeutic soaks.
